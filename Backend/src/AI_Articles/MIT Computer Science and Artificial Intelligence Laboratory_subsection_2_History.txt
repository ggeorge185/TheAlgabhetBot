Computing Research at MIT began with Vannevar Bush's research into a Differential analyser|differential analyzer and Claude Shannon's electronic Boolean algebra in the 1930s, the wartime MIT Radiation Laboratory, the post-war Whirlwind I|Project Whirlwind and Research Laboratory of Electronics (RLE), and MIT Lincoln Laboratory's Semi-Automatic Ground Environment|SAGE in the early 1950s. At MIT, research in the field of artificial intelligence began in late 1950s.
