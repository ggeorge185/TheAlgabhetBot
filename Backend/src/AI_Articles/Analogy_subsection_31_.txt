Islamic jurisprudence makes ample use of analogy as a means of making conclusions from outside sources of law. The bounds and rules employed to make analogical deduction vary greatly between Madhhab|madhhabs and to a lesser extent individual scholars. It is nonetheless a generally accepted source of law within Usul Al-Fiqh|jurisprudential epistemology, with the chief opposition to it forming the Zahiri|dhahiri (ostensiblist) school.
