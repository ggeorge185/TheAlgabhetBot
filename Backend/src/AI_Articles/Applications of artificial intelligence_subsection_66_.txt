AI has been used to optimize traffic management, which reduces wait times, energy use, and emissions by as much as 25 percent.


Smart traffic lights have been developed at Carnegie Mellon University|Carnegie Mellon since 2009.  Professor Stephen Smith has started a company since then Scalable Urban Traffic Control|Surtrac that has installed smart traffic control systems in 22 cities.  It costs about $20,000 per intersection to install. Drive time has been reduced by 25% and traffic jam waiting time has been reduced by 40% at the intersections it has been installed.
