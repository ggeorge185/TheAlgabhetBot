The English word ''creativity'' comes from the Latin term , and ''facere'' 'to create' and 'to make'. Its Morphological derivation|derivational suffixes also come from Latin. The word "create" appeared in English as early as the 14th century, notably in Chaucer (in The Parson's Tale), to indicate divine creation.

The modern meaning of ''creativity'' in reference to human creation did not emerge until after Age of Enlightenment|the Enlightenment.
