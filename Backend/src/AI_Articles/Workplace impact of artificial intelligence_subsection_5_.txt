AI is expected to lead to changes in the skills required of workers, requiring Retraining|training of existing workers, flexibility, and openness to change.  The requirement for combining conventional expertise with computer skills may be challenging for existing workers.

In the European Union, the General Data Protection Regulation, while oriented towards consumer data, is also relevant for workplace data collection.  Data subjects, including workers, have "the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing".  Other relevant Directive (European Union)|EU directives include the Machinery Directive (2006/42/EC), the Radio Equipment Directive (2014/53/EU), and the General Product Safety Directive (2001/95/EC).<ref name=":1" />
