In 2022, Musk refused to block Mass media in Russia|Russian state media on SpaceX's Starlink in the Russo-Ukrainian War|Starlink satellites in response to Russia's invasion of Ukraine, calling himself "a Freedom of speech|free speech absolutist." On April 14, 2022, Musk made an offer to buy Twitter, saying "I invested in Twitter as I believe in its potential to be the platform for free speech around the globe, and I believe free speech is a societal imperative for a functioning democracy".  Musk clarified his definition of free speech as "that which matches the law",  advocating that corporate censorship of speech should not go significantly beyond minimum legal requirements, but that lawful censorship requests should be upheld. In November 2022, Musk tweeted that he was so committed to free speech that he would not ban @ElonJet, a Twitter account that tracked the publicly available real-time flight data of Musk's private plane.
