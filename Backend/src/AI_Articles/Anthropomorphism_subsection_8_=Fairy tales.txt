Anthropomorphic motifs have been common in fairy tales from the earliest ancient examples set in a mythological context to the great collections of the Grimm's Fairy Tales|Brothers Grimm and Charles Perrault|Perrault. The ''Tale of Two Brothers'' (Egypt, 13th century BCE) features several talking cows and in ''Cupid and Psyche'' (Rome, 2nd century CE) Zephyrus, the west wind, carries Cupid and Psyche|Psyche away. Later an ant feels sorry for her and helps her in her quest.
