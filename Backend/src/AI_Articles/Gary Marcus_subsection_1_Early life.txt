Marcus was born into a Jewish family in Baltimore, Maryland. He developed an early fascination with artificial intelligence and began coding at a young age. 

Marcus majored in cognitive science at Hampshire College. He continued on to graduate school at Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where he conducted research on negative evidence in language acquisition and Regularization (linguistics)|regularization (and over-regularization) in children's acquisition of Morphology (linguistics)|grammatical morphology. 

During his PhD studies, he was mentored by Steven Pinker.
